Privacy Policy & Terms

Last updated: June 9, 2026

This Privacy Policy & Terms document explains how Shineflow ("Shineflow", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, protects, shares, and retains information in connection with Shineflow Amazon Seller Analytics, our service-enabled analytics application and reporting workflow for authorized Amazon Selling Partners ("clients", "Selling Partners", or "you").

This document also sets out the terms that govern use of our services. It covers data accessed through Amazon Seller Central and the Amazon Selling Partner API, also known as SP-API, including Amazon Brand Analytics data where the client is eligible and has authorized access.

Shineflow handles Amazon Selling Partner data in accordance with applicable laws, contractual obligations, and applicable Amazon policies.

Part 1 — Privacy Policy

1. Information from Amazon Seller Central and SP-API

When you authorize Shineflow, we may access and process Amazon Selling Partner data from your Amazon account through Seller Central and SP-API, within the SP-API roles approved by Amazon and the scope you grant.

This may include:

  • Amazon Selling Partner account and marketplace participation data
  • Catalog, listing, product content, variation, and A+ content data
  • Pricing, offer, buy box, promotion, sales, traffic, and marketplace performance data available through approved SP-API roles
  • Inventory, order metrics, FBA fulfillment, inbound shipment, shipment status, replenishment, and stock planning data
  • Finance, settlement, fee, reimbursement, transaction, and profitability-related data available through approved SP-API roles
  • Brand Analytics data, where available to eligible and authorized Selling Partners, including Amazon Search Terms, Market Basket Analysis, Repeat Purchase, Promotion Performance, and related Brand Analytics reports
  • Amazon Warehousing and Distribution data, where applicable, including AWD inventory summaries, inbound shipment status, shipment details, and reconciliation information for authorized Selling Partners using AWD

Shineflow accesses Amazon Selling Partner data only after authorization by the Selling Partner and only within the roles and permissions approved by Amazon and granted by the authorizing Selling Partner.

Shineflow does not request or use Amazon buyer personal information for marketing purposes. Our services are designed to support marketplace operations, analytics, reporting, and account management for the authorizing Selling Partner.

2. Information You Provide Directly

When you contact Shineflow, request a quote, submit a form, communicate with our team, or enter into a service agreement, we may collect information you provide directly, including:

  • Name
  • Company name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Job title
  • Marketplace information
  • Catalog size or ASIN count
  • Business inquiry details
  • Messages or documents you choose to provide

We use this information to respond to inquiries, prepare quotes, provide services, communicate with you, and manage our business relationship.

3. How We Use Information

Shineflow uses authorized Amazon Selling Partner data only to provide services to the authorizing Selling Partner and to operate, maintain, and support those services.

We may use information to:

  • Provide marketplace analytics, reporting, dashboards, exports, and business reviews
  • Monitor sales, traffic, and marketplace performance
  • Support catalog, listing, content, variation, and A+ content review
  • Review pricing, offer, buy box, fee, and promotion performance
  • Monitor FBA inventory, shipment status, replenishment needs, and fulfillment-related issues
  • Analyze inventory levels, sales velocity, order metrics, and stockout risks
  • Review settlement, fee, reimbursement, transaction, and profitability-related data
  • Provide Brand Analytics insights for eligible and authorized Selling Partners, including Search Terms, Market Basket Analysis, Repeat Purchase, and related reports
  • Monitor Amazon Warehousing and Distribution inventory and shipment information where applicable
  • Detect anomalies, operational issues, data gaps, and performance changes
  • Provide recommendations and account management support to the authorizing Selling Partner
  • Maintain security, troubleshoot technical issues, and support authorized workflows

We do not use Amazon Selling Partner data for any purpose unrelated to providing services to the authorizing Selling Partner.

4. How We Share Information

We deliver reports, analysis, dashboards, exports, and recommendations only to the Selling Partner that authorized access.

Shineflow does not:

  • Sell or resell Amazon Selling Partner data
  • Share one Selling Partner's data with another Selling Partner
  • Use one Selling Partner's data to provide services to another Selling Partner
  • Aggregate Selling Partner data across customers for resale or disclosure
  • Publish insights about Amazon's business or another Selling Partner's business
  • Use Amazon Selling Partner data for unrelated marketing purposes
  • Share Amazon Information with outside parties, except where required by law or with the client's express authorization

If Shineflow uses a third-party service provider to process non-Amazon contact or inquiry information, such as quote requests submitted through our website, that provider is required to process such information under confidentiality and data-protection obligations. Shineflow does not authorize such providers to sell the information or use it for their own marketing purposes.

5. Data Retention

Shineflow retains Amazon Selling Partner data only as long as necessary to provide the authorized services, meet legal or contractual obligations, resolve disputes, maintain security records, or support audit and compliance requirements.

Clients may request deletion of their data or removal of access by contacting jj@shineflowseattle.com.

Upon termination of services, the client should revoke or remove Shineflow's Amazon account permissions. Shineflow will delete or de-identify retained client Amazon data where feasible and legally permitted.

Where deletion is not immediately feasible due to legal, security, backup, audit, or technical requirements, Shineflow will restrict further processing and delete or de-identify the data when retention is no longer required.

6. Security Controls

Shineflow maintains administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect Amazon Selling Partner data against unauthorized access, use, alteration, disclosure, or loss.

These safeguards may include:

  • Limiting access to Amazon Selling Partner data to authorized personnel with a business need
  • Applying least-privilege access practices
  • Using role-based access controls where available
  • Using multi-factor authentication where available
  • Protecting data in transit using HTTPS, TLS, or other secure transmission methods where applicable
  • Handling credentials, tokens, and secret keys securely
  • Avoiding hard-coding credentials or secret keys into public repositories or application code
  • Reviewing access periodically and removing access when no longer needed
  • Maintaining internal security procedures for authorized personnel
  • Monitoring and investigating suspected unauthorized access or security events

Shineflow requests only the Amazon roles needed to deliver the authorized services.

7. Security Incident Response

Shineflow maintains an internal incident response plan for security events involving Amazon Selling Partner data and other protected business information.

The plan defines internal roles and responsibilities, escalation procedures, investigation steps, containment and recovery actions, evidence preservation, customer communication procedures, and post-incident review requirements.

Shineflow reviews and verifies its incident response plan at least every six months and updates the plan after major infrastructure changes, significant system changes, or lessons learned from security events.

If Shineflow detects a security incident involving Amazon Information, Shineflow will investigate the incident, take appropriate containment and remediation actions, preserve relevant evidence, and notify Amazon in accordance with Amazon's applicable data protection and incident reporting requirements.

8. Your Choices and Rights

Clients may request:

  • Removal of Amazon data access

Clients can revoke Shineflow's Amazon authorization through their Amazon account or contact Shineflow for assistance.
